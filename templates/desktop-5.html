Summarize the updates regarding speciality chemicals

The Restrictions Roadmap document includes several updates and planned restrictions regarding specialty chemicals. Key updates are summarized below:

Ortho-Phthalates (C4-C6)
Hazards: Reproductive, Endocrine Disruptors
Uses in Scope: Professional, Consumer, Articles
Details: There is an ongoing study to develop dossiers for around 40 C4-C6 ortho-phthalates. A group entry is being prepared by France, with a possible restriction extending the existing restriction on four phthalates. Expected dossier submission is planned for Q4 2025.
2. Hydrocarbyl Siloxanes
Hazards: PBT/vPvB (Persistent, Bioaccumulative, and Toxic/very Persistent and very Bioaccumulative)
Uses in Scope: Industrial, Professional, Consumer, Articles
The need for further regulatory management measures for hydrocarbyl siloxanes is being assessed by ECHA, Member States, and the Commission. Restrictions based on their PBT/vPvB properties are under consideration, with potential implementation not before 2025.
3. Bisphenols
Hazards: Endocrine Disruption
Uses in Scope: Industrial, Professional, Consumer, Articles
Details: Further regulatory action is being assessed, including restrictions or SVHC identification for bisphenols. This includes the work on the restriction dossier for bisphenol A and structurally related bisphenols.
4. Substances in Thermal Paper
Hazards: Endocrine Disruption (and possibly others)
Uses in Scope: Professional, Consumer, Articles
Details: Following the restriction of bisphenol A in thermal paper, there is ongoing monitoring of bisphenol S and other potential substitutes. The scope might overlap with bisphenols considered in other entries, but specific timing for restrictions is not yet decided.
5. Lead Chromate Pigments
Hazards: Carcinogenic, Reproductive
Uses in Scope: Articles
Details: A planned restriction under Article 69(2) targeting materials other than PVC, as the risks from these substances in PVC articles are covered by existing lead restrictions.

These updates reflect ongoing and future regulatory activities aimed at addressing the risks posed by specific groups of specialty chemicals. The restrictions are part of a broader effort to manage chemical safety and environmental health as per the EU’s Chemicals Strategy for Sustainability.
